The application offers an automatized method of colorizing black and white images. It performs best on small resolution photos of landscapes and sceneries.

How does it work?

To colorize a black and white image, the application uses a Convolutional Neural Network to predict the colorized version of the photo. It uses the Autoencoder architecture which consists of:

  • An encoder – which extracts low level features of the black and white image
  • A decoder – which tries to reconstruct the colorized image

To speed up the learning process the images are converted into the Lab color space, where the L channel is the input of the network and the ab channels are the output. The input and output are concatenated then converted back into RGB to obtain the result.

Deploying the model as a web application

After training the model, it was only I who could make use of it. In order for the colorizer to be useful to the public I had to deploy it to the internet.

I used the Google AI Platform on Google Cloud to deploy my model as an API. I then built a simple web app that can take pictures from users, colorize them using the service and then show the results on its front page gallery.
